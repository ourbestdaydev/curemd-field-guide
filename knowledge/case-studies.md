# CureMD Case Studies & Success Stories

> A vendor-neutral digest of publicly available CureMD case studies, client testimonials, and vendor-asserted performance metrics. **Every outcome figure below originates from CureMD's own marketing materials or a client quote inside that material; none has been independently audited.** Vendor-asserted figures are marked as such, and depth is assessed honestly per study. Source URLs are cited inline.

## How to read this
CureMD publishes three tiers: (a) named-client case studies with some operational detail, (b) short testimonial blurbs, and (c) aggregate marketing metrics not tied to any named client. This guide separates them. Where CureMD itself attaches a disclaimer ("results may vary"), that is noted.

## Named-client case studies

### 1. Davis County Health Department — Public Health
- **Client / specialty:** Davis County Health Department (Utah). Public/community health agency with home-visitation nursing and field teams.
- **Challenge:** Paper-heavy intake and immunization logs; scheduling disconnected from billing (long waits, missed charges); no patient portal; limited remote access for field nurses; no integration with state registries for communicable-disease and tuberculosis tracking.
- **Adopted:** Cloud EHR with auto-populating templates; integrated scheduling + billing ("Smart Scheduling"); patient portal with secure messaging; mobile/remote charting for home visits; real-time eligibility checks; daily claim submission; integration with the Utah Statewide Immunization Information System and lab systems.
- **Outcomes (vendor-asserted):** 60% reduction in administrative time; 95% staff satisfaction; 20% increase in patient satisfaction; qualitatively faster reimbursements, outbreak response, and contact tracing.
- **Quotes (Davis County):** "CureMD's ability to provide real-time insurance eligibility checks has helped staff identify insurance coverage issues quickly… the option to send claims daily has sped up our billing — we are receiving payments more quickly than before." / "Be open-minded to change… the more you know about CureMD, the more likely you'll see how it can be customized to work for you."
- **Source:** https://www.curemd.com/pdf/public-health-case-study.pdf (also referenced at https://www.curemd.com/case-studies.asp)
- **Depth:** The most substantive study in the set — structured, named, names the specific state-registry integration, two attributed quotes. The three headline percentages remain vendor-asserted with no methodology shown.

### 2. Genesis Internal Medicine — Internal Medicine
- **Client / size:** Solo provider (Dr. Ahsen Sheikh), Geneseo, NY; ~4 staff, ~2,000 patients; a brand-new all-electronic practice (opened Jan 2009).
- **Challenge:** Stand up a paperless practice from day one with a web-based, easy system including e-prescribing and lab ordering.
- **Adopted:** EHR + Practice Management; e-prescribing; lab ordering. Implemented in a few weeks; hour-long online training sessions.
- **Outcomes (vendor-asserted):** ~$10,000/year saved by repurposing chart-storage space into an additional exam room (space/throughput-driven, not billing-driven).
- **Quote:** "A computer has become almost like a medical tool, like a stethoscope."
- **Sources:** https://www.curemd.com/pdf/genesis-internal-medicine.pdf ; https://blog.curemd.com/curemd-helps-genesis-internal-medicine-save-10000-a-year/
- **Depth:** Mid-tier — real named physician, plausible narrative, but only one soft financial estimate (avoided storage space, not a measured revenue figure).

### 3. Yassin Pediatrics — Pediatrics
- **Client / size:** One physician, 10 staff, ~1,500 visits/month; Lacey Township, NJ.
- **Challenge:** Long-time paper practice, price-sensitive, hesitant about health IT; wanted affordable and scalable.
- **Adopted:** CureMD All-in-One (EHR, Practice Management, Medical Billing Services) + Patient Portal. Started on Enterprise Practice Management, upgraded to All-in-One within nine months. 20 hours online training; dedicated account manager; live in 3.5 weeks. Uses e-fax, e-prescribing, enterprise scheduling, and real-time KPI dashboards (denials, revenue, co-pay).
- **Outcomes (vendor-asserted):** 23% saved on operational expenditure via paperless workflow; no additional staff needed; billing/rebilling "less than a minute"; faster claim payment.
- **Quote (Nagwa S. Awad):** "Because of CureMD, we get more accomplished with less manpower, which frees up our staff to tend to patients."
- **Source:** https://www.curemd.com/PDF/yassin-pediatrics.pdf
- **Depth:** Mid-tier — real practice with concrete size, timeline, and one operational-cost figure (23%); billing claims qualitative.

### 4. Hope Cancer Clinic — Oncology
- **Client:** Independent oncology practice (Dr. Harmesh Naik), Livonia, MI.
- **Challenge:** Legacy EMR couldn't keep pace; reporting took "several hours"; large accounts-receivable balances; manual transaction tracking.
- **Adopted:** All-in-One EHR + Practice Management; Revenue Cycle Management Services; oncology/chemotherapy management (regimen library, clinical decision support and safety alerts, automated dosage calculations, adverse-event documentation per the National Cancer Institute's Common Terminology Criteria, charge capture, e-prescribing, imaging integration).
- **Outcomes:** No quantified metrics — qualitative only (reports now "within seconds," automatic denial notifications, supported growth).
- **Source:** https://www.curemd.com/case-studies/oncology.asp
- **Depth:** Specialty-rich, metric-poor — strong oncology feature fit, but the revenue-cycle problems it sets up are never closed with a figure.

### 5. Dr. Alan J. Dayan — Orthopedic Surgery
- **Client:** Solo orthopedic surgeon (adult joint reconstruction); client for five years at publication.
- **Challenge:** Patient volume doubled, creating administrative bottlenecks; wanted to go paperless quickly with an affordable, customizable specialty system.
- **Adopted:** Orthopedic EHR; Pulmonary Function Test module; "smart phrases"; patient portal with online payment.
- **Outcomes:** None quantified.
- **Source:** https://www.curemd.com/case-studies/orthopedics.asp
- **Depth:** Testimonial-grade — establishes specialty fit and relationship longevity, no measurable outcomes.

## What these case studies reveal
1. **The strongest, most repeated value claim is revenue-cycle/billing improvement — but the hard numbers live in the *unnamed* examples.** The cleanest financial figures (28→10-day turnaround / 22% revenue; 42.3%→53.8% collections) are anonymized; named clients speak about billing qualitatively or give soft, non-billing savings.
2. **Efficiency and "doing more with less staff" is the most consistent *named-client* theme** (Davis County 60% less admin time; Yassin 23% lower operating cost, no added staff; Genesis reclaimed an exam room).
3. **Specialty fit and the integrated all-in-one stack are genuine differentiators** in the qualitative record (oncology chemotherapy tooling; orthopedic templates; public-health registry integration). Consolidating EHR + Practice Management + billing into one platform is repeatedly cited as the reason clients switched.
4. **Implementation speed and support recur as satisfaction drivers** (Genesis "a few weeks"; Yassin 3.5 weeks + dedicated account manager + 20 hours training).
5. **Newer products (AI Scribe, Leap Health) barely appear** — the published case-study library lags CureMD's current product marketing.

### Honest note on verifiability
None of the outcome figures is independently verifiable; every percentage and dollar amount is a client's own statement inside a CureMD-authored document or a CureMD aggregate claim, and the cleanest-looking numbers come from *anonymized* examples flagged "results may vary." The body of work supports the **qualitative** conclusions (efficiency, specialty fit, integrated stack, onboarding) far more strongly than any specific number. Before relying on headline percentages, ask CureMD for a named reference in your own specialty and size band.
